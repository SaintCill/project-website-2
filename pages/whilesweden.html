Before Traveling:

Packing:

If you are heading to Sweden during winter, especially northern Sweden,
you are going to need to pack like you are going on a skiing trip.
During January - February you can expect to encounter temperatures
as low as -30°C or -22°F if you are American.

Packing for such weather is essential if you are planning on leaving your hotel.
Those of you who live in warmer climates, where such winter clothing is harder to get,
don't fret, there is an alternative.

If you are unable to get a hold of some clothes that will keep you warm
in northern Sweden's temperatures, I suggest setting aside some additional spending
cash. There are many cheap stores with quality winter clothing here in Sweden. We'll be
listing some below.

Once You Get Here...

Shopping for Essentials:

So you made it to northern Sweden, you've checked into your hotel and now you are ready to
tackle the northern tundra. Great! Now is the time to check for a few essentials if you got a hold
of winter clothes before arriving. First and most important would be your shoes. If you are wearing
sneakers or something similar, I would recommend getting some winter boots. You will also want some
thick socks, preferably wool. Trust me, you will regret going out if you don't have proper footwear.

Check below for more details!

In General:

In General:

For your hands, I recommend some winter gloves. Even if you don't wear them everyday, they are going to be
the MVP of the trip if you have to use your hands while out and about. A thick scarf might also be in
your shopping list if you didn't bring one, but this is more dependent on the weather. During the early
winter months, as well as March, you will get away with not wearing one. During the colder months however,
you will be swearing like a pissed off Viking if you don't have one.

Also get a proper winter hat, or your complaining will annoy the rest of the group.

For Explorers:

For Explorers:

Before heading out and exploring the tundra, you are going to want to get some long johns. If you are
planning on going out into nature, the long johns will provide the first of three layers against the
cold. That is right, three layers. It might seem excessive, but take the word of someone who has lived
here for years, you would rather be a bit too hot than freezing. Even if you are just wandering through
trails, you might encounter a situation that requires you to wade through snow, so at least bring them
with you if you are planning on going outside. Snowshoes are optional. They are handy, but I've walked my
way through snow-covered forests more than once without snowshoes, so it's really up to you if you want to
bring some.
